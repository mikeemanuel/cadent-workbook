Brand Overview

Our mission

At Cadent, we believe technology should work for the business, not the other way round. We help small and medium enterprises digitise their operations, automate the work that slows them down, and build the digital foundations they need to grow with confidence.

Our vision

To give every growing business access to enterprise-grade digital capability, right-sized, affordable and built to last. We want SMEs to compete on technology, not be held back by it.

Brand essence

Cadent is a digital transformation partner, not just an adviser. We sit at the intersection of strategy, technology and execution, turning ambition into working systems. Our name reflects steady, deliberate momentum, the rhythm of a business moving forward.

Logo Usage

Our logo is the most recognisable expression of the brand. Use it consistently and protect it.

Reversed version (left) for deep navy and dark photography. Dark version (right) for white and light backgrounds. The icon keeps its cyan-to-blue gradient in both.

Primary mark

The Cadent logo pairs an ascending bar motif, suggesting growth, momentum and structure, with the Cadent wordmark and the "Consulting" descriptor. The icon bars run from cyan to blue, the brand's signature gradient direction. This is the default representation of the brand.

Reversed (white) version

The supplied artwork is the reversed version: a white wordmark with the cyan-to-blue icon, designed for dark navy and photographic backgrounds. This is the primary in-context lockup, since the brand leads with dark surfaces.

Clear space

Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the icon. No other graphic element, text or image should intrude on this protected area. This keeps the mark legible and uncluttered.

Minimum size

To preserve legibility, never reproduce the full logo smaller than 120px wide for digital use, or 30mm wide for print. Where space is tight, use the icon on its own.

Background usage

The reversed logo is built for the brand's deep navy and for dark photography. On white or light surfaces, use the dark version (assets/cadent-logo-dark.png), the wordmark set in Cadent Ink (#030A26) with the icon keeping its cyan-to-blue gradient. Always ensure strong contrast; never place the logo on a busy or low-contrast background.

Typography

Cadent uses a single, modern typeface for clarity and consistency: Inter.

Primary typeface — Inter

Inter is a clean, highly legible sans-serif designed for screens. It carries the whole brand, from bold display headings to body copy and small labels. Use weights 400 to 800; avoid mixing in other typefaces.

Display — Inter ExtraBold 800

Typography guidelines

  • Lead with bold, confident headlines. Short statements outperform long ones.
  • Use the cyan accent on a single key word in a headline, not the whole line.
  • Keep body text in Slate or Ink. Never set long passages in cyan or blue.
  • Maintain generous line spacing (1.6) for body copy and comfortable reading.
  • Use SemiBold uppercase eyebrows to introduce sections, in Electric Blue or Cyan.
  • For emphasis, use weight (Bold), not italics or underlines.
  • Where Inter is unavailable (e.g. some email clients), fall back to Arial or system sans-serif.

Tone of Voice

Cadent sounds like a sharp, trusted partner: confident, clear and focused on results.

Brand personality

We speak with the authority of people who have done the work, and the plain language of people who want to be understood. We are confident without hype, expert without jargon, and always anchored to the outcome for the client's business.

Voice attributes

ConfidentWe state outcomes plainly and back them with evidence. No hedging, no hype.
ClearWe make complex technology simple. Plain words, short sentences, no jargon.
Outcome-focusedWe lead with business results, revenue, efficiency, ROI, not features.
PartneringWe work alongside clients. "We don't just advise, we help implement."
PragmaticEnterprise-grade thinking, right-sized for a growing business and a real budget.
Forward-lookingWe talk about momentum and what's next, scalable, secure, built to last.

Writing style

  • Use British English spelling throughout (digitise, optimise, programme, organisation).
  • Write in short, punchy sentences. Break up long paragraphs.
  • Lead with the benefit to the business, then explain the how.
  • Use active voice: "We deploy and integrate your systems," not "Your systems are integrated."
  • Speak to the reader as "you" and "your business." Refer to ourselves as "we" / "Cadent."
  • Use concrete numbers where you have them. Specific beats vague.
  • Avoid filler and consultancy clichés (synergy, leverage, paradigm, best-in-class).

Imagery & Graphics

Cadent's visual style is modern, technical and premium, dark surfaces, clean product imagery and confident data.

Style

Lead with deep navy backgrounds and crisp, well-lit product and device imagery, dashboards, mobile screens, analytics. Use cyan glow and gradient accents sparingly to create depth and focus.

Data & dashboards

Charts and dashboards are core brand imagery, they show transformation in action. Keep them clean, legible and on-palette, using cyan and blue for key data points against dark surfaces.

Iconography

Use simple, line-style icons with a consistent stroke weight and rounded ends. Render icons in Electric Blue or Cyan, often inside a soft circular tint. Avoid detailed or illustrative icon styles.

Photography

When using people, choose authentic, professional SME settings, real teams and workplaces, not generic corporate stock. Apply a cool, slightly contrasty grade that sits comfortably beside the navy palette.

Principles

  • Dark-first: design for deep navy surfaces with bright accents, not the reverse.
  • Premium and uncluttered: generous space, clean edges, subtle shadows.
  • Show outcomes: favour imagery that implies growth, momentum and results.
  • Stay on-palette: avoid off-brand colours in graphics, charts and overlays.
  • Use the ascending-bar motif from the logo as a recurring graphic device.

Digital Applications

How the brand comes together across the website, social media and email.

Website

The Cadent website leads with a deep navy hero, a bold display headline with a single cyan accent word, and a concise value statement. Content sections alternate between white and Mist backgrounds, with dark navy banners reserved for high-impact moments such as the stats strip and calls to action.

  • Primary buttons use Electric Blue (#006DE2) with white text, rounded corners (10px) and a soft brand shadow; on hover they lift slightly.
  • Cards sit on white or Mist with a 1px Border and a subtle shadow.
  • Navigation stays simple: 5–6 primary items plus a single primary call to action ("Book a Consultation").
  • Use cyan underlines or accents to mark the active or hovered state.

Social media

  • Use the reversed logo on a navy or gradient background as the profile image.
  • Apply the hero gradient as a backdrop and the accent gradient for highlights.
  • Keep typography to Inter; lead posts with a bold, single-idea headline.
  • Mix thought leadership, client outcomes and clear explainers of each service line.

Email

  • White background with the logo in the header on a navy band.
  • A thin accent-gradient bar can sit beneath the header.
  • Body text in Arial or a system sans-serif for client compatibility.
  • Calls to action mirror the website button style: Electric Blue, white text, rounded.

Do's and Don'ts

Quick rules to protect the integrity of the Cadent brand.

Logo

Do
  • Use the approved logo artwork.
  • Keep the required clear space around it.
  • Place it on dark navy or high-contrast backgrounds.
  • Scale it proportionally.
  • Use the dark-text wordmark on light surfaces.
Don't
  • Stretch, skew or distort the logo.
  • Add shadows, outlines or effects.
  • Rotate, flip or recolour the icon gradient.
  • Place the reversed logo on light or busy backgrounds.
  • Recreate or redraw the mark.

Brand application

Do
  • Use the brand palette consistently across every touchpoint.
  • Follow the type hierarchy and stick to Inter.
  • Lead with confident, outcome-focused, British-English copy.
  • Reserve the accent gradient for high-impact moments.
  • Design dark-first with clean, premium layouts.
Don't
  • Introduce off-brand colours or extra typefaces.
  • Overuse the gradient or apply it to body text.
  • Use jargon, hype or unsupported claims.
  • Crowd layouts or use low-contrast text.
  • Use generic, off-palette stock imagery.
